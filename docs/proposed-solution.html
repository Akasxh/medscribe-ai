MedScribe AI — HACKMATRIX 2.0 Proposed Solution

The Problem

India's healthcare system serves over 1.4 billion people, yet its clinical workforce remains critically understaffed. The doctor-to-patient ratio stands at roughly 1:1,000, well below the WHO recommendation of 1:1,000 in urban areas and far worse in rural settings. In this high-pressure environment, physicians spend an estimated 2–3 hours every day on clinical documentation—writing case notes, filling discharge summaries, coding diagnoses, and preparing referral letters. This administrative burden directly reduces the time available for patient care, accelerates burnout, and contributes to India's growing physician attrition crisis.

Compounding the problem is India's unique linguistic landscape. Doctor-patient conversations routinely occur in Hindi-English code-mixed speech—a patient might describe their symptoms as "pet mein dard ho raha hai, 3 din se fever bhi hai" while the doctor responds with clinical English interspersed with Hindi reassurances. Global ambient scribe tools (Abridge, Nuance DAX, Suki) are built for monolingual English clinical settings and fail catastrophically on code-mixed Indian conversations. Meanwhile, India's ambitious Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM) mandates FHIR-compliant health records for interoperability, yet no affordable tool exists that can convert a spoken consultation into standards-compliant digital records. Rural telemedicine expansion—a national priority—remains bottlenecked by the documentation overhead imposed on already-overstretched physicians.

Our Solution

MedScribe AI is a mobile-first Progressive Web App that transforms spoken doctor-patient conversations into structured clinical notes and FHIR R4–compliant resources in real time. The workflow is radically simple: a doctor opens the app on their phone, taps a single record button, and speaks naturally with their patient in any mix of Hindi and English. As the conversation flows, MedScribe AI streams a live transcript while simultaneously extracting chief complaints, diagnoses with ICD-10 codes, medications with dosage and RxNorm mappings, vitals coded to LOINC, allergies, and a structured SOAP note—all updated incrementally on screen.

What sets MedScribe AI apart is the integration of Clinical Decision Support directly into the documentation pipeline. As medications and allergies are extracted, the system checks for drug-drug interactions, allergy cross-reactivity, and dosage safety—raising real-time alerts before the prescription is finalized. A Differential Diagnosis Engine suggests alternative diagnoses with supporting evidence and distinguishing tests. Every consultation produces a scored FHIR R4 Bundle (Grade A–D) ready for ABDM integration, complete with Patient, Encounter, Condition, Observation, MedicationRequest, AllergyIntolerance, CarePlan, and ServiceRequest resources. The system learns continuously: when a doctor corrects an extraction, that correction is stored and injected as a few-shot example into future calls, improving accuracy without model retraining. Zero API cost for speech recognition—we leverage the browser's native Web Speech API—makes this viable even in resource-constrained settings.

Data Flow

The browser captures speech via the Web Speech API (supporting Hindi + English), streams recognized text over a WebSocket to the FastAPI backend. Accumulated transcript segments are batched every 10–15 seconds and sent to Gemini 2.5 Flash with a structured clinical extraction prompt. The AI returns structured JSON which is mapped to FHIR R4 resources, scored for completeness, and run through the CDS engine for safety checks. All results stream back to the frontend over the same WebSocket connection.

MedScribe AI — HACKMATRIX 2.0 Key Features

Key Features

Feature 01

Hindi-English Code-Mixed Understanding

Natively handles conversations where doctors and patients switch freely between Hindi and English. Extracts clinical entities from phrases like "pet mein dard" (abdominal pain) and maps Indian drug brand names (Crocin, Combiflam) to their generic equivalents with correct RxNorm codes.

Feature 02

Clinical Decision Support (CDS)

Real-time safety engine with 10 drug-drug interaction rules, 3 allergy cross-reactivity checks, and 10 dosage validation rules. Catches dangerous combinations (e.g., Warfarin + Aspirin, NSAID in renal impairment) before the prescription is finalized. Severity-graded alerts (high / moderate / low).

Feature 03

FHIR R4 + ABDM Compliance

Generates a complete FHIR R4 Bundle with 8 resource types: Patient, Encounter, Condition (ICD-10), Observation (LOINC), MedicationRequest (RxNorm), AllergyIntolerance, CarePlan, and ServiceRequest. Each resource is quality-scored (Grade A–D) for coding completeness and inter-resource referential integrity.

Feature 04

Differential Diagnosis Engine

For every primary diagnosis, the AI suggests 2–3 alternative diagnoses with supporting evidence extracted from the conversation and recommended distinguishing tests. Helps doctors consider conditions they might overlook, supporting diagnostic rigor and reducing misdiagnosis rates.

Feature 05

Continuous Learning from Corrections

Doctors can edit any extracted clinical note field and click "Save & Teach AI." Corrections are stored and injected as few-shot examples into future Gemini extraction calls. The system improves with use—no model fine-tuning or retraining required. Accuracy compounds across consultations.

Feature 06

Export Suite & Demo Mode

One-click export to print-ready clinical notes (opens browser print dialog), FHIR Bundle JSON download, and clipboard copy. Built-in demo mode with 3 pre-loaded scenarios (Viral Fever, Diabetes Follow-Up, Cardiac + Safety Alerts) ensures reliable presentations even without live microphone access.

Projected Clinical Impact

  • Time savings: Reduces documentation time from ~15 minutes to ~1 minute per consultation, freeing 2+ hours/day for direct patient care.
  • Patient safety: Real-time CDS catches dangerous drug interactions and allergy cross-reactivity before prescriptions are finalized—errors that manual review often misses under time pressure.
  • Digital health readiness: Automatic FHIR R4 output means every consultation is ABDM-ready from day one, accelerating India's health record interoperability goals.
  • Equity: Browser-native speech recognition with zero API cost makes ambient scribing accessible to small clinics and rural telemedicine setups, not just well-funded hospital chains.
  • Diagnostic quality: Differential diagnosis suggestions and risk factor identification support clinical reasoning, especially for junior doctors and GPs handling unfamiliar presentations.
